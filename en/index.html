La Vojo

The Symbol

We did not come here to obey a master.
We came here to learn to see.

To see what?

That life does not belong to us — it passes through us.
That we are made of flesh, of consciousness, and of mystery.
That the mystery is not beyond the world —
it is at the heart of every encounter.

The way is simple: know yourself, and you will know the other.
Love the other, and you will reach what does not die.

No one owns this way.
It is open to all who walk.

The five precepts

The Vojo does not demand perfection — it asks for lucidity.
The precepts are not commandments whose breach condemns —
they are orientations whose breach instructs.

  • Know yourself.
  • Do not do to another what you would not wish to receive.
  • Care first for the vulnerable.
  • Take only what you need.
  • Respect that in which the Logos dwells.

What we do

We read ancient texts together — Thomas, John, Mary Magdalene — in translations made directly from the original manuscripts into Esperanto.

We walk. We eat together.

Come if this speaks to you.

The four feasts

  • La Festo de la Vojo (the feast of the Way) — autumn equinox — 23 September 2026
  • La Festo de la Karno (the feast of the Flesh) — winter solstice — 21 December 2026
  • La Festo de la Vido (the feast of Sight) — spring equinox — 20 March 2027
  • La Festo de la Lumo (the feast of Light) — summer solstice — 21 June 2027
